# TechCorp Product Management Career Ladder

## Overview

TechCorp uses a competency-based career framework with 6 levels for Product Management. Progression is based on demonstrating competencies at the next level, not time in role.

**Current Level:** L4 - Senior Product Manager
**Target Level:** L5 - Staff Product Manager

---

## Level Definitions

### L3 - Product Manager
*Foundation building*
- Owns well-scoped features end-to-end
- Writes clear PRDs with guidance
- Partners effectively with eng/design
- Conducts basic user research

### L4 - Senior Product Manager (Current)
*Independent ownership*
- Owns entire product area or major initiative
- Drives complex cross-functional projects
- Makes product strategy decisions independently
- Influences roadmap priorities
- Mentors junior PMs

### L5 - Staff Product Manager (Target)
*Strategic leadership & influence*
- Defines multi-quarter product strategy
- Influences company-level product decisions
- Drives alignment across multiple teams
- Recognized thought leader internally
- Builds and scales product practices

### L6 - Principal Product Manager
*Company-wide impact*
- Sets product vision for entire company
- Defines new market opportunities
- Builds product culture and practices
- External thought leadership

---

## Competency Framework - L4 to L5

### 1. Product Strategy (20%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Defines roadmap for one product area
- Uses data and research to inform priorities
- Aligns with company strategy

**L5 (Target):**
- Defines multi-product or platform strategy
- Identifies new market opportunities
- Influences company-level strategy decisions
- Thinks 12-18 months ahead

**Gap:** Need to demonstrate platform-level thinking, not just feature-level. Show influence on broader product portfolio decisions.

---

### 2. Execution Excellence (20%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Ships complex projects on time
- Makes scope/timeline trade-offs
- Handles single-team coordination

**L5 (Target):**
- Ships initiatives spanning multiple teams
- Navigates ambiguity and competing priorities
- Unblocks complex organizational challenges
- Consistently delivers high-impact work

**Gap:** Need examples of multi-team coordination. Mobile launch is good, need 1-2 more.

---

### 3. Cross-Functional Leadership (20%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Partners with immediate team (eng/design)
- Presents to leadership occasionally
- Resolves team-level conflicts

**L5 (Target):**
- Leads cross-functional initiatives across org
- Influences exec decision-making regularly
- Builds strong relationships across all functions
- Drives alignment in ambiguous situations

**Gap:** Strong with Jordan/Maya. Need more evidence of exec influence and sales/marketing partnership.

---

### 4. Customer & Market Insight (15%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Conducts user research for own features
- Analyzes usage data
- Maintains customer relationships

**L5 (Target):**
- Develops deep market expertise
- Translates insights into strategy
- Builds systems for ongoing customer learning
- Influences product vision with insights

**Gap:** Good customer relationships (Acme Corp). Need to show how insights shaped strategy.

---

### 5. Technical Judgment (10%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Understands technical trade-offs
- Partners with engineering on feasibility
- Makes informed technical decisions

**L5 (Target):**
- Drives architectural decisions
- Identifies technical opportunities/risks early
- Influences technical strategy
- Deeply understands platform/systems

**Gap:** Need to show architectural influence, not just feature-level technical decisions.

---

### 6. Communication & Influence (10%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Writes clear PRDs and updates
- Presents roadmap to stakeholders
- Gains buy-in from immediate team

**L5 (Target):**
- Influences through written narratives
- Presents to board/investors
- Gains buy-in across organization
- Recognized internal thought leader

**Gap:** Need examples of written strategy docs. No board/investor exposure yet.

---

### 7. Team Development (5%)

**L4 (Current):**
- Mentors junior PMs
- Shares knowledge informally
- Participates in hiring

**L5 (Target):**
- Raises the bar for entire PM team
- Builds product processes/practices
- Develops other senior PMs
- Drives hiring strategy

**Gap:** Good 1:1 mentorship. Need to show team-level impact (processes, practices).
