## What I Learned

### About career progression
The gap between L4 and L5 is clearer now. It's not about working harder - it's about:
- Broadening influence (not just eng/design bubble)
- Thinking platform-level (not just feature-level)
- Executive communication (strategic narratives, concise updates)
- Multi-team leadership (cross-functional coordination)

Mobile launch gives me opportunities to demonstrate all of these.

### About decision-making
The timeline decision with Jordan taught me something: when engineering is worried about technical risk, they want to know that *I* understand the risk, not that we'll eliminate it. Once I demonstrated understanding of push notification risk and accepted it, Jordan was comfortable moving forward.

### About cross-functional partnerships
Reaching out to Sales felt awkward initially (we don't usually collaborate), but Mike was welcoming. Lesson: Don't let past patterns limit future opportunities. Most people are open to partnership if you make the first move.

### About platform thinking
Lisa's API partnership conversation shifted my perspective. Mobile isn't just an app for our users - it's a platform for ecosystem partners. This opens new strategic opportunities and is exactly the kind of big-picture thinking required for L5.

### About executive communication
The stakeholder update formula that worked: clear status, key decisions, what I need from them. No fluff, no over-explaining. CEO responded positively. Keep practicing this level of communication.
